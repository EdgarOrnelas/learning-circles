Master new skills, together, in the library, for free.

Attend an info session to learn more and sign up Edgewater: 6:30PM Tuesday, April 21 in the Community Meeting Room (2nd Floor) Harold Washington: 6:30PM Wednesday, April 22 and Thursday, April 23 in the over the rainbow

Chicago Public Library is hosting study groups for learners who want to complete online courses together, in-person at the libraries.
These are free, peer-supported courses which are designed to be taken by anybody.

FAQ

When does it start?

Courses begin during the week of April 27. There will be two hours of group study time each week in the library.

How long will it take?

The courses will run for between five-seven weeks. However, the pace is largely up to you. You can always slow down or speed up - the materials are there to be used and you can use them however you want.

How will this all go down?

Every week you’ll join your study group for a meeting of minds (and regular high fives) in your local library branch. You’ll be guided through the online materials by a community organiser, who will help facilitate collaboration with your fellow learners.

What will I learn?

Every week you’ll join your study group for a meeting of minds (and regular high fives) in your local library branch. You’ll be guided through the online materials by a community organiser, who will help facilitate collaboration with your fellow learners.

What do I need to bring?

All you need is a few hours per week in your library branch and a thirst for knowledge. The library will provide computers, and all necessary course materials.

Why should I take a course?

Why not? Sure, each course will teach you a new skill, but even more exciting is the chance to join with other people from your community and learn something new together in the library. We believe that learning can, and should, be fun, and we hope that you will join us. You’ll receive a certificate from P2PU and CPL at the end of the course.
