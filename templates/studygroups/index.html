FAQ

When does it start?

Courses begin during the week of April 27. There will be two hours of group study time each week in the library.

How long will it take?

The courses will run for between five-seven weeks. However, the pace is largely up to you. You can always slow down or speed up - the materials are there to be used and you can use them however you want.

How will this all go down?

Every week you’ll join your study group for a meeting of minds (and regular high fives) in your local library branch. You’ll be guided through the online materials by a community organiser, who will help facilitate collaboration with your fellow learners.

What will I learn?

Each course has been selected in the hope that it is interesting and relevant to you and the people in your study group. As well as the informational content, we’ve designed the course time so that participants gain experience giving and receiving feedback feedback, working in groups, and feeling comfortable working online.

What do I need to bring?

All you need is a few hours per week in your library branch and a thirst for knowledge. The library will provide computers, and all necessary course materials.

Why should I take a course?

Why not? Sure, each course will teach you a new skill, but even more exciting is the chance to join with other people from your community and learn something new together in the library. We believe that learning can, and should, be fun, and we hope that you will join us. You’ll receive a certificate from P2PU and CPL at the end of the course.

About

Chicago Public Library is hosting study groups for learners who want to complete online courses together, in-person at the library.These are free, peer-supported courses which are designed to be taken by anyone, with no prerequisites necessary.

P2PU and Chicago Public Library believe that people learn better when they’re learning together, and we’ve designed the study groups to provide the maximum support from the library and project staff as you work your way through some of the best free online educational content on the Web. As you move through the course, you’ll be able to share your progress, questions and successes with your study group, and provide support for others.

The courses on offer have been carefully selected to be interesting and relevant to library users, and will lead to developing useful skills that can be applied in the online and offline worlds. Learners can choose to join any of the study groups.
